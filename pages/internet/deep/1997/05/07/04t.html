Popularized by Microsoft's Windows 95, directory trees are designed to assist in the mass-brainwashing of those who fail to grasp the much simpler 'ls' command. You've likely seen these distinctive trees, immediately recognizable by their "+" and "-" nodes to open/close subdirectory listings. I invariably find myself clicking every "+" and never returning to the "-", but perhaps my desire to clearly see all available information is unusual. But I digress.
